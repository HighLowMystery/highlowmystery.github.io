Hi, I’m Alex

I’m Alex Sabr, the person behind ErgoRemote. I’m an ergonomics enthusiast, a home office specialist, and someone who’s spent the last 5+ years working remotely in a real day-to-day setup — not a showroom, not a test lab, and definitely not a content farm pretending to have used the gear.

ErgoRemote exists because I learned the expensive way that bad office gear can wreck your workday. I bought chairs that looked good in photos and felt awful after a week. I used setups that made my neck tighten up by late afternoon and my lower back ache by the end of the month. Eventually I stopped trusting marketing pages and started testing everything with one question in mind: would I actually want to use this for eight hours a day?

That’s still how I review gear now.

My Background

My background is practical, not corporate. I’m a long-term remote worker based in Canada, and most of my review process comes from living with this equipment every day:

  • full workdays at a desk instead of five-minute impressions
  • real comparisons between chairs, desks, laptop stands, and monitors
  • attention to posture, comfort, adjustability, and long-session fatigue
  • buying and testing products the way normal remote workers actually do

I focus on the gear that matters most for people building a better home office: ergonomic chairs, standing desks, monitors, laptop stands, docking gear, and everyday desk essentials.

Why I Review Ergonomic Gear

A lot of product content in this space is written to rank first and help second. That never sat right with me.

I review ergonomic gear because comfort and function compound over time. A monitor that’s slightly too low might not seem like a big deal on day one, but after months of work it matters. A chair with weak lumbar support might feel fine for 20 minutes and terrible by week three. Those are the details I care about, because those are the details that actually affect remote workers.

When I recommend something on ErgoRemote, I’m trying to answer the question I wish someone had answered for me earlier: is this worth putting in your home office, or is it just well marketed?

What You Can Expect From ErgoRemote

My goal is simple:

  • write in plain language
  • stay honest about tradeoffs
  • prioritize long-term comfort over hype
  • recommend products that make sense for real budgets

I care about trust. If something is overpriced, poorly designed, or only looks good in product shots, I want that to be obvious.
