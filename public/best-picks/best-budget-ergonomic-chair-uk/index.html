What to Look for in a Budget Ergonomic Chair (UK Edition)

The non-negotiables

Adjustable lumbar support. Height adjustment minimum. Depth adjustment is better. A fixed bump is better than nothing but won’t work for everyone.

Seat height range. UK desks are typically 72–75cm. Confirm the chair’s height range covers you with feet flat on the floor.

CE and UK safety certification. Cheap import chairs sometimes skip this. Every chair on this list carries appropriate UK market certification.

The nice-to-haves

4D armrests — adjust height, width, pivot, and depth. Particularly useful if you switch between keyboard and mouse work frequently.

Seat depth adjustment — lets you set the correct 2–3 finger gap between seat edge and back of knee regardless of your leg length.

Recline lock — ability to lock the recline at a specific angle (usually 90°, 110°, 125°). Useful if you alternate between focused work and more relaxed reading/calls.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best ergonomic chair under £200 in the UK?

The SIHOO M57 at around £189 on Amazon UK. It has adjustable lumbar height, a breathable mesh back, and a moveable headrest — more than most chairs at this price can offer. The trade-off is 1D armrests (height only) and no lumbar depth adjustment.

Are cheap ergonomic chairs worth it?

At £150 and under, usually not — the lumbar mechanisms tend to be fixed bumps rather than adjustable supports. At £180–200, options like the SIHOO M57 become genuinely worthwhile. The step-change in real ergonomic function happens around the £200–250 mark.

Is it worth buying a used Herman Miller in the UK?

Yes, and the UK has a strong secondhand market for them through eBay UK and specialist office furniture dealers. A used Aeron (size B, the most common) runs £300–500 and still outperforms most new chairs at twice the price. Check for frame cracks and confirm the PostureFit mechanism engages smoothly before buying.

Do UK ergonomic chairs include VAT in listed prices?

Amazon UK prices include VAT for consumer purchases. If you're buying as a business and are VAT-registered, you can usually reclaim the VAT element. For chairs over £250, this can represent a meaningful saving.

How long should a budget ergonomic chair last?

A decent budget chair (£180–300) should last 3–5 years with regular use. Chairs in this range typically use lower-grade cylinder mechanisms and foam that compresses faster than premium chairs. The Flexispot C7 and Hbada E3 Pro are the most likely to approach the upper end of that range.
