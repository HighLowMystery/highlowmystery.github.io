What to Look for in an Under-Desk Treadmill

1. Belt stability

A shaky belt kills the experience fast. If the treadmill feels twitchy underfoot, your typing suffers and you’ll stop using it.

2. Real noise level

Marketing copy loves the word quiet. What matters is whether you can take a Zoom call without sounding like you’re vacuuming the room.

3. Low-speed control

The best working speed is much slower than most people think. You want smooth control in the roughly 0.6 to 2.0 mph range, not top-end bragging rights.

4. Deck height

The higher the treadmill deck, the more desk height range you need. If your standing desk already maxes out early, a tall treadmill can ruin the ergonomics.

5. Storage and footprint

If you live in a smaller Canadian apartment or a multi-use room, portability matters. Some treadmills are easy to slide away; others are effectively permanent furniture.

Speed Range Guide

This is where most people get it wrong.

  • 0.6 to 1.0 mph: Best for typing-heavy work, email, admin, and calls
  • 1.0 to 1.5 mph: Best for lighter typing, reading, and heads-down work
  • 1.5 to 2.0 mph: Good for podcasts, meetings where you’re mostly listening, and non-typing tasks
  • 2.0+ mph: Usually too fast for serious desk work unless you’re just watching or listening

My rule: if your shoulders are bouncing enough to make typing annoying, you’re going too fast. Productivity drops before cardio benefits do.

Are Under-Desk Treadmills Actually Worth It?

For the right person, yes.

If you’re expecting a treadmill desk to replace workouts, probably not. If you want to reduce the sheer amount of sitting you do during a workday, they’re absolutely worth considering. The biggest benefit is not fat loss magic — it’s that you stop spending 8-10 hours completely still.

I think under-desk treadmills make the most sense for remote workers who:

  • already use a standing desk regularly
  • want more movement without leaving the desk
  • work from home full-time
  • struggle with winter inactivity or long indoor stretches

That last one is real. In Canada, movement friction is high for a good chunk of the year. A treadmill under the desk removes a lot of excuses.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you actually work while walking?

Yes, but only at the right speed. For most people, 0.8 to 1.2 mph is the sweet spot for typing, email, Slack, and low-intensity focus work. Faster than that and your accuracy starts to drop.

What speed is best for productivity?

Usually between 0.8 and 1.5 mph. Use the lower end for writing and typing, and the higher end for meetings, reading, and lighter admin work.

Are under-desk treadmills worth it?

They are worth it if your goal is reducing sitting time during the workday, not replacing real workouts. The best ones help you move more without disrupting work.

What is the best under-desk treadmill for small spaces?

The Goplus 2-in-1 is the best option here because it folds and stores more easily than heavier office-grade treadmill bases. If you want an even slimmer budget pick, UREVO is the other strong option.

Do under-desk treadmills work with standing desks?

Yes, but desk height range matters. A treadmill raises your standing position, so shorter desks or shakier budget frames can become a problem. Pairing one with a stable sit-stand desk makes a huge difference.
