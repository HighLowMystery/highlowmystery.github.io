What Makes the Best Ergonomic Chair for Back Pain?

1. Lumbar support should be adjustable - both in height and (where possible) depth. Your spine is unique; support must match your curve and position.

2. Mesh or foam: personal preference, but airflow matters - If you run hot, mesh is best. If you value plush comfort and don’t mind warmth, foam is fine (Branch is a good example).

3. Look for seat depth and recline adjustability - Both aid in maintaining lumbar contact.

4. Armrests should meet elbows at 90°, adjust up/down at minimum

5. Test fit where possible - If you can, sit and try before buying. Failing that, make sure return policy is generous.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the #1 cause of back pain from office chairs?

Lack of adjustable lumbar support — most office chairs have none, or a fixed bump that can worsen pain for some people.

Should I get mesh or padded seats for back pain?

Mesh keeps you cooler and tends to maintain support shape longer. Padded seats can be more comfortable at first but may compress. For back pain, lumbar fit is more important than seat material.

What's the best chair under $300 for back pain relief?

The SIHOO M57 at $279. It has real lumbar height adjustment, mesh support, and is a major upgrade over standard office/kitchen chairs.

Are secondhand premium chairs worth it?

Absolutely if bought from a reputable dealer. Used Aerons and Leap V2s still outperform most new chairs at half the price. Check for warranty and frame integrity.

Does chair fit or monitor height matter more?

For back pain, chair fit (especially lumbar contact) is king — but monitor height is #2. Low screens force forward-head posture, which loads the whole spine including lower and mid back. Ideally fix both.
