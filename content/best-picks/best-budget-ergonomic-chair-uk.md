## What to Look for in a Budget Ergonomic Chair (UK Edition)

### The non-negotiables

**Adjustable lumbar support.** Height adjustment minimum. Depth adjustment is better. A fixed bump is better than nothing but won't work for everyone.

**Seat height range.** UK desks are typically 72–75cm. Confirm the chair's height range covers you with feet flat on the floor.

**CE and UK safety certification.** Cheap import chairs sometimes skip this. Every chair on this list carries appropriate UK market certification.

### The nice-to-haves

**4D armrests** — adjust height, width, pivot, and depth. Particularly useful if you switch between keyboard and mouse work frequently.

**Seat depth adjustment** — lets you set the correct 2–3 finger gap between seat edge and back of knee regardless of your leg length.

**Recline lock** — ability to lock the recline at a specific angle (usually 90°, 110°, 125°). Useful if you alternate between focused work and more relaxed reading/calls.
