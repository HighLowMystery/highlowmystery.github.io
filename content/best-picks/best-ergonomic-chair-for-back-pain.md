## What Makes the Best Ergonomic Chair for Back Pain?

**1. Lumbar support should be adjustable** - both in height and (where possible) depth. Your spine is unique; support must match your curve and position.

**2. Mesh or foam: personal preference, but airflow matters** - If you run hot, mesh is best. If you value plush comfort and don't mind warmth, foam is fine (Branch is a good example).

**3. Look for seat depth and recline adjustability** - Both aid in maintaining lumbar contact.

**4. Armrests should meet elbows at 90°, adjust up/down at minimum**

**5. Test fit where possible** - If you can, sit and try before buying. Failing that, make sure return policy is generous.
