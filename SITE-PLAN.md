## 📝 Content Strategy

### Post Type 1: "Best X for Y" (Primary Traffic Driver)
**Format:**
- Title: "Best [Product Category] for [Specific Person/Use Case] in [Year]"
- Length: 2,000–3,500 words
- Structure:
  1. Hook (personal angle — 1–2 paragraphs, real experience)
  2. Quick picks table (top 3–5 products)
  3. Who this guide is for
  4. Full reviews (300–500 words each, pros/cons)
  5. Buying guide (what to look for)
  6. FAQ (3–5 questions, targets People Also Ask)
  7. Final verdict

**Examples:**
- Best Ergonomic Chairs Under $500 (2025)
- Best Monitors for Remote Work Under $300
- Best USB-C Hubs for Home Office

### Post Type 2: "X vs Y" Comparisons (High Conversion)
**Format:**
- Title: "[Product A] vs [Product B]: Which Is Right for You?"
- Length: 1,500–2,500 words
- Structure:
  1. Quick answer (for people who just want the TL;DR)
  2. Head-to-head specs table
  3. In-depth comparison (ergonomics, price, durability, aesthetics)
  4. Who should buy X vs who should buy Y
  5. Final recommendation
- **Key:** Always give a clear winner. Wishy-washy = low conversions.

**Examples:**
- Flexispot E7 vs Uplift V2 Standing Desk
- Herman Miller Aeron vs Secretlab Titan Chair
- Ultrawide Monitor vs Dual Monitor Setup

### Post Type 3: "How to X" (SEO Authority + Email Capture)
**Format:**
- Title: "How to [Solve Pain Point] When Working From Home"
- Length: 1,000–2,000 words
- Structure:
  1. Identify the problem (empathy-first)
  2. Step-by-step solution
  3. Product recommendations embedded naturally (not forced)
  4. Summary + CTA (email list or related review post)
- **Goal:** Build authority and internal link equity; convert readers to the review posts

**Examples:**
- How to Set Up a Home Office for Under $1,000
- How to Reduce Back Pain When Working From Home
- How to Choose the Right Monitor Size for Your Desk

## 🧑‍💻 Humanization Plan (Critical — Must Execute Every Post)

### The Problem We're Solving
AI-written content fails because it:
- Uses the same sentence length throughout (robotic rhythm)
- Has no specific details (dates, prices, personal experiences)
- Uses hollow filler phrases ("In conclusion", "It's worth noting")
- Sounds like it's covering all bases rather than making real decisions
- Never admits uncertainty or trade-offs honestly

### Rules for Every Post

#### 1. Reading Level: 9th Grade
- Use Hemingway App (hemingwayapp.com) to check every post before publish
- Target score: 6–9 (Grade 6–9 reading level)
- Replace: "utilize" → "use", "purchase" → "buy", "optimal" → "best"
- Short sentences dominate. Mix in one long sentence occasionally for rhythm.

#### 2. Personal Anecdotes (Non-Negotiable)
Every post must include at least ONE of:
- "I've been working from home for [X] years and..."
- "When I first tried [product], I noticed..."
- "My back used to kill me until I switched to..."
- "My setup went through three iterations before I got it right..."
- Specific details: the date you bought something, the price you paid, how long you've used it

#### 3. Sentence Rhythm (The Human Tell)
- **Bad:** "The chair has good lumbar support. It also has adjustable armrests. The seat depth is also adjustable."
- **Good:** "The lumbar support is the real selling point here. Honestly? My lower back stopped aching within a week. The armrests adjust too, which is nice, but that's table stakes at this price point."

#### 4. Honest Trade-offs
- Every product has a flaw. Name it. "The cable management is a joke." "Setup took me 3 hours."
- Never 5-star everything. It reads as paid content.
- Include: "I wouldn't recommend this if you..."

#### 5. Forbidden Phrases (Never Use These)
- "In conclusion"
- "It's worth noting that"
- "In this article, we will explore"
- "Look no further"
- "Game-changer" (overused to death)
- "Dive into"
- "Delve into"
- "Seamlessly"
- Any sentence starting with "Overall, "

#### 6. Voice & Tone
- Write like you're texting a friend who asked for advice
- Opinions are allowed. Required, actually.
- Use contractions: "don't", "it's", "you'll"
- Use em dashes for asides — like this — instead of parentheses
- Numbers under 10: spell them out. 10+: use numerals.
